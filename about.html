SCON - Society for Conservation of Nature

Dedicated to the conservation of vultures and the preservation of our ecosystems.

About Us

SCON (Society for Conservation of Nature) is a dedicated non-profit organization working towards the conservation of vultures and wildlife. We understand the crucial role vultures play in maintaining a balanced ecosystem by preventing the spread of diseases and reducing environmental contamination.

Through our efforts, we are committed to ensuring a healthy, sustainable future for these important birds and their habitats.

Our Mission

Our mission at SCON is to promote the conservation of vultures and other endangered species across the globe. By focusing on awareness campaigns, community outreach programs, and direct conservation efforts, we aim to safeguard the future of these vital creatures.

Key Objectives

  • Wildlife Awareness: Raise comprehensive awareness about the vital ecological role of vultures and their critical conservation needs through targeted campaigns and educational initiatives.
  • Community Education: Provide extensive education to local communities about the multifaceted benefits of wildlife conservation, emphasizing the interconnectedness of healthy ecosystems.
  • Rescue & Rehabilitation: Initiate immediate rescue operations for injured, sick, or orphaned vultures and implement comprehensive rehabilitation programs for successful release back into their natural habitats.
  • Strategic Partnerships: Collaborate with governments, international organizations, and local stakeholders to establish and protect critical habitats of endangered species through policy advocacy and conservation agreements.
  • Scientific Research: Conduct in-depth research on vulture populations, migration patterns, breeding behaviors, and emerging threats to develop evidence-based conservation strategies.
  • Anti-Poaching Efforts: Support and implement local initiatives to prevent illegal poaching, poisoning, and habitat destruction while promoting sustainable coexistence between human activities and wildlife.
  • Capacity Building: Train local conservationists, veterinarians, and community leaders to create sustainable, long-term conservation networks across vulnerable regions.
  • Policy Advocacy: Advocate for stronger environmental policies and legislation that protect vultures and their ecosystems while promoting biodiversity conservation at national and international levels.

Our Projects

Some of our key projects include:

  • Vulture Awareness Campaigns: Spreading awareness about the importance of vultures to communities and policymakers.
  • Vulture Rescue Programs: Coordinating with local wildlife hospitals to rescue and rehabilitate vultures.
  • Habitat Protection Initiatives: Working with conservation agencies to protect critical vulture habitats.
  • Educational Workshops: Educating schools and local groups on the importance of preserving vultures and biodiversity.
